Ignacio Cid

PhD Candidate in Criminology | University of Lausanne

I am a PhD candidate in Criminology at the University of Lausanne. My research lies at the intersection of criminology, sociology, and spatial analysis, with a particular focus on lethal violence in Latin America. I am especially interested in homicide trends, spatial concentration of crime, subnational inequalities, and the macro-social and cultural factors associated with violence.

About

My work combines quantitative methods, spatial analysis, and comparative research. I have broader interests in sociology of religion, public security, Latin American studies, and data visualization.

Research interests

  • Homicide and lethal violence in Latin America
  • Spatial concentration of crime
  • Comparative and subnational criminology
  • Sociology of religion and violence
  • Quantitative methods, spatial methods, and data visualization

Research

Homicide in Latin America
Comparative and subnational analysis
I study regional and subnational variation in homicide, with special attention to long-term trends, concentration, and internal heterogeneity within countries.
Homicide in Chile
Municipal-level and spatial approaches
My work on Chile examines the territorial concentration of homicide, municipal-level predictors of lethal violence, and the spatial clustering of high-risk areas.
Religion and violence
Culture, context, and violent outcomes
I also explore the relationship between religion, culture, and violence, including the contextual effects of religious environments on violent outcomes.
Methods
Quantitative and spatial methods
My work uses multilevel models, logistic and count models, spatial autocorrelation, comparative designs, and data visualization.
